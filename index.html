What We've Done

Transformations that prove the science.

Three landmark projects that show what becomes possible when precision agronomy meets committed farmland.

Flagship Project
01

Reviving the Mango Orchard

A 12-acre mango orchard in Anand district had been yielding less than 30% of its potential for seven consecutive seasons — the result of soil acidification, compaction, and depleted micronutrient profiles. Our team conducted a full soil audit across 9 sampling zones, rebuilt the pH balance through targeted lime and vermicompost programs, introduced beneficial mycorrhizal fungi, and redesigned the drip irrigation layout. Within two seasons, the orchard returned to peak Kesar mango production — trees flowering uniformly, fruits developing full size and sweetness. The farmer saw a 3× increase in marketable yield.

03

Barren Land → Agro Tourism Destination

A 6-acre plot in Sabarkantha with severely eroded, saline topsoil — written off by its owner as unsalvageable — became one of Gujarat's first boutique agro-tourism retreats. Terrava designed a phased soil reclamation plan, introduced salt-tolerant pioneer crops to rebuild structure, then layered in ornamental edible gardens, a shaded farm pavilion, and a seasonal visitor program. Today the land hosts farm stays, school field trips, and a weekend harvest market. What was once a financial liability now generates year-round income through agriculture and experience tourism combined.
